The Idea

Together, we make a better forecast

Users Contribute Observations

Forecast users report the conditions using any means available; from complex sensors to human observation.

We Re-Run the Forecast

Using the latest user observations, we update the forecast using our supercomputer and redistribute it to the users.

You Get Credit for Your Observations

If your observations improved the accuracy of the forecast, you get website credit. Everybody gets a better forecast.

About

Our humble beginnings

  • 2005-2012

    Offshore Wind Resource Assessment

    Our founder was burried away in a dark office writing a dissertation about the offshore wind enregy potential of most of the Unitd States, running weather models at high-resolution to create detailed wind fields.

  • 2013

    America's Cup Forecast in San Francisco Bay

    Based on research to simulate harmful turbulence in wind farms, we began forecasting for the 34th America's Cup. We found that we often knew more about the winds on the course the America's Cup competitors and figured we might be on to something.

  • Fall 2013/Winter 2014

    PDF Forecasts over Email

    Based on positive feedback from early users, we woke up early on yacht racing days to run the forecast and send it out to hundreds of users over email. Popularity eventually necessitated the automation of the forecast... because people wanted it nearly every Saturday and Sunday.

  • March 2014

    SailTactics.com Goes Operational

    We gave users what they wanted, a more detailed and accurate sailing forecast than they had ever seen for San Francisco Bay.

  • 2014 and 2015

    Our Users Won Nearly Every Race

    Our forecast users won nearly every major sailing race in 2014 and 2015. The results don't lie, we created a new and useful forecast that gave our users a competitive advantage.
